Why Roblox is a great place to start

Roblox Studio is free, simple to install, and full of templates you can learn from. You can build an obby, a small simulator, or a hangout map with basic scripts. You do not need advanced coding to publish your first experience. Start small, test often, and improve as you go.

How to create a Roblox game

1. Install Studio and sign in

  1. Install Roblox Studio from the official Roblox site. Sign in with your account.
  2. Open Studio and look at the templates. Baseplate is a clean start, Obby shows useful parts, and Village gives a quick world to explore.

2. Learn the basic tools

  1. Use Move, Scale, and Rotate to shape your map.
  2. Anchor parts that should not fall. Group parts that belong together.
  3. Add SpawnLocation so players spawn where you want.

3. Add simple gameplay

  1. Insert a Part and make it a collectible. Use a simple Script to add coins or score.
  2. Place checkpoints for obbies. Color them so they stand out.
  3. Test with Play in Studio. Fix gaps, adjust jumps, and remove stuck spots.

4. Publish and share

  1. Click File, then Publish to Roblox.
  2. Set game icon, description, and genre. Make the experience public when ready.
  3. Invite friends to test. Watch how they play and note where they struggle.
Keep scripts simple at first. If you are curious about Lua, start with variables, events, and functions. Copy a small snippet, test it, then add one new idea at a time.
